Frequently Asked Questions

What is IceDip?

IceDip is a cold plunge timer that tracks your cold water immersion sessions. It features five science-based benefit zones that show you the physiological changes happening in real time as you progress through your session.

What are Benefit Zones?

As you stay in cold water, your body passes through five zones: Cold Shock (0–30s), Adaptation (30–60s), Dopamine Zone (60–120s), Metabolic Boost (120–180s), and Deep Resilience (180s+). Each zone represents different physiological responses. You can customize the zone thresholds in Settings.

How does Apple Health integration work?

IceDip can save your cold plunge sessions as workouts in Apple Health. This is completely opt-in (disabled by default) and write-only — the App saves workout data but never reads your Health data. You can enable or disable it in Settings > Health, and revoke access at any time via iOS Settings > Health > IceDip.

How does iCloud sync work?

Your session data syncs automatically across your Apple devices via iCloud using SwiftData. The data is stored in your personal iCloud account, encrypted and managed by Apple. The developer has no access to your data.

How do I delete my data?

Go to Settings > Data > Delete All Data to permanently remove all sessions and reset all preferences. You can also remove HealthKit data via iOS Settings > Health > IceDip, and iCloud data via iOS Settings > iCloud > Manage Storage.

Does IceDip track me?

No. IceDip contains no analytics, no advertising, no tracking services, and no third-party SDKs. Your data stays entirely on your devices and in your personal iCloud account.

What languages are supported?

IceDip is available in English, German, Spanish, French, Japanese, and Turkish.

What devices are supported?

IceDip requires iOS 17.0 or later for iPhone. The companion Watch app requires watchOS 10.0 or later.

How do Watch sessions sync?

Sessions completed on your Apple Watch sync automatically to your iPhone via WatchConnectivity when your devices are paired. No external servers are involved.

How do Siri Shortcuts work?

You can say "Start my cold plunge in IceDip" to begin a session with your default duration, or specify a custom duration like "Start a 3-minute cold plunge in IceDip". Set up shortcuts in the Shortcuts app or via Settings > Siri > IceDip.
